Alternator or Starter? Know the Signs

Failing alternator:

  • Battery or charging warning light on the dash
  • Dim or flickering headlights, especially at idle
  • A battery that keeps dying even after replacement
  • Whining or growling from the front of the engine

Failing starter:

  • A single click (or rapid clicking) when you turn the key
  • Grinding during start, or intermittent no-starts
  • The engine cranks slowly even with a healthy battery

Parts stores swap parts; we test. A full charging-system test tells us whether it's the battery, alternator, starter, or a bad connection, so you don't pay for parts you didn't need.

Alternator & Starter FAQs

Is it my battery or my alternator?

If a jump start gets you going but the car dies while driving, it's likely the alternator. If the car starts fine once running but won't crank after sitting, suspect the battery or starter. Our test pinpoints it in minutes instead of guessing.

How long does alternator replacement take?

Most vehicles are done the same day. Some engines bury the alternator and take longer. We'll tell you up front when we quote it.

Why did my new battery die again?

A weak alternator undercharges the battery, and a parasitic draw drains it overnight. Either one will kill a brand-new battery. Testing the whole system is the only way to break the cycle.
