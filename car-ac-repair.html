Car AC Repair That Blows Cold Again

Our car AC repair service finds the real reason your vents are blowing warm — whether it's a refrigerant leak, a failing compressor, or an electrical fault — and fixes it right the first time.

Common Car AC Problems We Fix

  • Warm air from the vents, even with the AC on max
  • Weak airflow or air that only blows from some vents
  • AC that cools at highway speed but not in town
  • Musty or moldy smells when the AC runs
  • Clicking, rattling, or squealing when the compressor kicks on

A cheap "recharge" without finding the leak is just renting cold air — the refrigerant escapes the same way it did before. We diagnose the system first so your repair actually lasts.

Car AC Repair FAQs

Why does my AC recharge keep running out?

Because there's a leak. Refrigerant doesn't get "used up" — a properly sealed system holds its charge for years. If you're recharging every season, you're paying for the same refrigerant over and over instead of fixing the leak once.

How long does car AC repair take?

Diagnosis and recharge are usually same-day. Compressor or condenser replacement typically takes a day depending on parts availability for your vehicle.

Why does my AC smell musty?

Moisture on the evaporator core grows mildew over time, especially in our damp Oregon climate. A cleaning treatment and fresh cabin filter usually solves it.
