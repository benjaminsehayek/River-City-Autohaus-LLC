Car Battery Replacement in Columbia County, Oregon

Our car battery replacement service includes a free charging system test, so you'll know whether you need a new battery or if a failing alternator is the real culprit.

Call (503) 867-0609

Signs Your Battery Is Dying

  • Slow, labored cranking, especially on cold, damp mornings
  • Needing a jump start more than once
  • White or green corrosion on the battery terminals
  • Radio presets and clock resetting themselves
  • A battery more than 4–5 years old

Oregon's cool, wet climate is easier on batteries than desert heat, but age still wins. We test your battery's actual cold-cranking capacity, not just voltage, so you replace it only when it's truly done.

Battery Replacement FAQs

How long do car batteries last?

Typically 4–6 years in the Pacific Northwest. Lots of short trips, common around town in Columbia County, shorten battery life because the alternator never fully recharges it.

Is the battery test really free?

Yes. It takes a few minutes, and it also tells us if your alternator is undercharging, which would kill a new battery just as fast as the old one.

Why does my battery keep dying?

If replacements keep failing, the battery usually isn't the problem. A weak alternator or a parasitic electrical draw is. We test for both before selling you another battery.
