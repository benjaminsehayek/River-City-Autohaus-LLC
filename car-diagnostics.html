Car Diagnostics & Check Engine Light Service

Our car diagnostics go beyond pulling a code — we trace check engine lights and electrical gremlins to the actual failed component so you only pay to fix what's broken.

Call (503) 867-0609

When to Get a Diagnostic

  • Check engine light on — steady or flashing
  • ABS, airbag, traction control, or battery warning lights
  • Stalling, hesitation, hard starts, or rough idle
  • Electrical gremlins: dead outlets, flickering lights, random warnings
  • Failed DEQ emissions test and you need to know why

A code tells you which system complained — not which part failed. An oxygen sensor code, for example, is often caused by a vacuum leak or exhaust leak, not the sensor. Pinpoint testing is the difference between fixing the car and throwing parts at it.

Car Diagnostics FAQs

The parts store read my code for free — why pay for a diagnostic?

The free scan reads the code; the diagnostic finds the cause. Replacing parts by code alone is how people spend $400 on sensors for a $20 vacuum leak. Our diagnostic fee covers actual testing — and we apply it toward the repair.

My check engine light is on but the car runs fine. Can I ignore it?

A steady light won't strand you today, but it can hide a problem quietly damaging your catalytic converter or fuel economy — and it blocks a DEQ pass. A flashing light means active misfire: get it checked immediately.

How much does a diagnostic cost?

We charge a flat diagnostic fee and quote it up front. If you have us do the repair, the diagnostic time is applied to the job.

If the diagnosis points to a mechanical failure, we handle that too — from engine repair to transmission repair and exhaust work.
