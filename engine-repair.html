Symptoms of Serious Engine Trouble

  • Knocking, ticking, or rattling from under the hood
  • White, blue, or heavy black smoke from the exhaust
  • Losing oil or coolant with no visible leak
  • Repeated overheating or coolant mixing with oil
  • Persistent misfires, rough idle, or major power loss

Before we quote any major engine work, we run compression and leak-down tests so you know exactly what's wrong. If a repair doesn't make financial sense for your vehicle, we'll tell you that too.

Engine Repair FAQs

Is my engine worth fixing?

It depends on the repair cost against the vehicle's value and condition. A $2,500 repair on a solid truck you own outright often beats a $600 monthly car payment. We'll walk through the math with you honestly — even when the answer costs us the job.

Rebuild, remanufactured, or used engine — which is best?

A used engine is cheapest up front, a remanufactured unit carries the strongest warranty, and a rebuild keeps your original block. The right call depends on your vehicle, budget, and how long you plan to keep it.

How long does major engine work take?

Head gaskets and timing work typically take 2–4 days. Rebuilds and replacements run about a week depending on machine shop and parts lead times. We'll give you a realistic timeline before we start.

Check engine light on but the engine seems fine? Start with car diagnostics. Overheating is often a cooling-system problem — see radiator repair. For worn drive belts, visit belt replacement.
