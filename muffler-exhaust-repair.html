Signs of an Exhaust Problem

  • Your vehicle suddenly got loud, or keeps getting louder
  • Rattling or banging under the car over bumps
  • Exhaust smell inside the cabin (get this checked immediately)
  • Hissing or ticking that changes with engine speed, usually a leak
  • A dragging or hanging pipe, or a failed DEQ emissions test

Exhaust leaks aren't just a noise problem. They can pull carbon monoxide into the cabin and cause failed emissions tests. Small leaks are usually a quick, inexpensive fix if you catch them early.

Muffler & Exhaust FAQs

Can I drive with an exhaust leak?

Short distances, usually, but you shouldn't wait. Leaks ahead of the oxygen sensors hurt fuel economy and can trigger the check engine light, and any leak can let exhaust fumes reach the cabin.

Weld it or replace it?

If the metal around the damage is solid, a weld or patch section is a cost-effective fix. If the pipe is rusted thin, welding is throwing money at metal that's about to fail. We'll show you the pipe and let you decide.

Do you replace catalytic converters?

Yes, including for theft victims. We install compliant converters and can add theft-deterrent shields so it doesn't happen twice.
