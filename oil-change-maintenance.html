Oil Change & Maintenance FAQs

How often should I change my oil?

Most modern vehicles on full synthetic go 5,000–7,500 miles between changes; older engines and lots of short trips call for sooner. We'll set your interval based on how you actually drive, not a one-size-fits-all sticker.

Is full synthetic oil worth it?

Usually, yes. It protects better in cold starts, resists breakdown, and stretches your interval — and many newer engines require it. For older, high-mileage engines, a high-mileage blend can be the smarter buy.

Can you do my maintenance without voiding my warranty?

Absolutely. The Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act protects your right to have maintenance done at any qualified shop. Keep your receipts — we'll keep records on our end too.

Spotting oil drips on the driveway? That's a repair, not a top-off — see engine repair. We can also rotate your tires with every oil change — see tire repair & service.
