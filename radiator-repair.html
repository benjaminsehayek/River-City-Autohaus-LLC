Cooling System Warning Signs

  • Temperature gauge climbing higher than normal
  • Green, orange, or pink puddles under the vehicle
  • A sweet smell from the engine bay, or steam from under the hood
  • Heater blowing cold when the engine is warm, often a sign of low coolant
  • Constantly topping off the coolant reservoir

Every overheat event risks a warped head or blown head gasket. A few hundred dollars of cooling repair protects you from thousands in engine work. If your gauge is climbing, pull over and call us.

Radiator Repair FAQs

Can I keep driving if my car is running hot?

No. This is one of the few problems where continuing to drive can total the engine. Pull over, let it cool, and have it towed or call us for advice before driving further.

Does stop-leak actually work?

It can slow a tiny leak temporarily, but it also clogs radiators and heater cores. It's a get-home trick, not a repair, and it often makes the eventual fix more expensive.

How often should coolant be changed?

Most vehicles call for a flush every 5 years or 100,000 miles, sooner for older systems. Old coolant turns acidic and eats gaskets and metal from the inside out.
