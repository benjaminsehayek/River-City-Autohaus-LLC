Signs Your Transmission Needs Attention

Transmission problems rarely fix themselves, and the longer you wait, the more expensive they get. Bring your vehicle in if you notice:

  • Slipping gears or the engine revving without accelerating
  • Shuddering or jerking during shifts
  • Delayed engagement when shifting into Drive or Reverse
  • Red or brown fluid spots under your vehicle
  • A burning smell or a check engine light with transmission codes

Many "failing transmissions" turn out to be a fluid, solenoid, or sensor issue that costs a fraction of a rebuild. That's why we diagnose first and never quote a rebuild until we know you actually need one.

Transmission Repair FAQs

Should I rebuild or replace my transmission?

It depends on your vehicle's age, mileage, and value. A rebuild reuses your original case with new internal components; a replacement swaps in a remanufactured or used unit. We'll price both options and tell you honestly which makes sense, including when the right answer is neither.

How much does transmission repair cost?

Minor repairs like solenoids, leaks, or fluid service often run a few hundred dollars. Full rebuilds are a bigger investment, which is why we always start with a diagnosis. You should never pay rebuild money for a sensor problem.

Is it safe to drive with a slipping transmission?

No. Slipping generates heat, and heat is what destroys transmissions. Continuing to drive can turn a repairable problem into a full rebuild. Call us and we'll help you decide whether it's safe to drive in or needs a tow.
